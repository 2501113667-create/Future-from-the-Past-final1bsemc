The Second Generation: Smaller, Faster, and More Reliable.

Key Developments: The invention of the transistor revolutionized electronics. Transistors replaced bulky vacuum tubes, making computers significantly smaller, faster, cheaper, and more reliable.

Major Inventions

  • Invention of the Transistor (1947):Developed at Bell Labs.
  • TRADIC (1954): The first fully transistorized computer.
  • Development of High-Level Programming Languages: Languages like FORTRAN (1957) and COBOL (1959) made programming more accessible.

Notable Figures: John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, William Shockley (inventors of the transistor).

Impact:Computers moved from research labs into businesses and universities. The increased reliability and reduced cost marked the beginning of the commercial computer industry.
