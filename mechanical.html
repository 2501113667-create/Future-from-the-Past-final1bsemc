The Dawn of Automated Calculation

Key Developments: This era began with the invention of mechanical calculators. The focus was on using gears, levers, and punch cards to perform calculations and automate tasks.

Major Inventions

  • Pascaline (1642): Blaise Pascal invented the first mechanical calculator.
  • Difference Engine & Analytical Engine (1800s): Charles Babbage designed these early mechanical computers, introducing the concept of a programmable machine.
  • Punch Card System (1890): Herman Hollerith used punch cards to tabulate the U.S. census, leading to the founding of IBM.

Notable Figures: Blaise Pascal, Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, Charles Babbage, Ada Lovelace (often called the first programmer), Herman Hollerith.

Impact: "These inventions demonstrated that machines could perform mathematical calculations faster and more conveniently than humans, laying the groundwork for future data processing industries."
