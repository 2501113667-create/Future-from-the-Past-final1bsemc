The Third Generation: Miniaturization and the Rise of the Minicomputer.

Key Developments: Multiple transistors and other electronic components were miniaturized and placed on a single silicon chip, called an integrated circuit (IC).

Major Inventions

  • Invention of the Integrated Circuit (1958-1959): Independently developed by Jack Kilby and Robert Noyce.
  • IBM System/360 (1964): A family of computers with a unified architecture, allowing customers to upgrade without rewriting software.
  • Rise of Minicomputers: Smaller, more affordable computers (like the DEC PDP series) brought computing power to smaller departments and labs.

Notable Figures: Jack Kilby, Robert Noyce, Gordon Moore.

Impact:Computing power became much more compact and energy-efficient. The standardization of hardware and software lowered costs further, making computers more widespread in business and industry.
