The Fourth Generation and Beyond: Personal Computing and the Internet Age.

Key Developments: Very Large-Scale Integration (VLSI) allowed thousands, then millions, of transistors to be fitted onto a single microprocessor chip.

Major Inventions

  • Microprocessor (1970): Intel introduced the 4004, the first commercial microprocessor.
  • Personal Computer The Altair 8800 (1975), Apple II (1977), and IBM PC (1981) brought computing into homes and offices.
  • Graphic User interface (GUI):Made computers user-friendly for non-experts (e.g., Apple Macintosh in 1984, Windows).
  • Internet and World Wide Web: Connected computers globally, revolutionizing communication and information access.
  • Mobile Computing and AIThe rise of smartphones, tablets, cloud computing, and artificial intelligence defines the current landscape.

Notable Figures: Ted Hoff, Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, Bill Gates, Tim Berners-Lee.

Impact:This era has democratized access to information and computing power. It has fundamentally transformed communication, commerce, entertainment, and virtually every aspect of modern society and industry, creating a globally connected world.
