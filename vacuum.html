Vacuum Tubes Era

Home Mechanical Era Vacuum Tubes Era Transistors Era Integrated Circuit Era PC & VLSI Era

The First Generation of Electronic Computers.

Key Developments: Computing shifted from mechanical parts to electronic vacuum tubes, which acted as switches and amplifiers.

Major Inventions

  • ABC (Atanasoff-Berry Computer) (1942): The first electronic digital computing device.
  • ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) (1946) The first general-purpose electronic digital computer, which was massive, consumed enormous amounts of power, and filled an entire room.

Notable Figures: John Atanasoff, Clifford Berry, John Mauchly, J. Presper Eckert.

Impact:This era introduced electronic speed to calculation, enabling complex scientific and military computations (like artillery trajectory tables) that were previously impossible. However, the machines were large, unreliable, and expensive.
